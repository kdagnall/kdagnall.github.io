PRESS RELEASE:
WINTERS PLEIN AIRE FESTIVAL

The Winters Plein Aire Festival hired me in 2012 to earn advance media coverage for their event and help attract tourism to the charming town of Winters, California.

Press releases are written to spark interest among journalists and decision makers at the media outlets you want to cover your story. There's a proper style and formatting to a news release that's specific to this kind of communication, and breaking the rules can be detrimental to your success and reputation.

This one worked as planned. We earned a ton of advance coverage for the art festival from TV, radio, online and print outlets, including the front page of the Sac Bee, California's fifth largest newspaper.

PRESS RELEASE:
BERRYESSA GAP GOLD MEDALS

When Berryessa Gap Vineyards executed a perfect sweep at the World Wine Tasting Championship, they were understandably overflowing with pride and wanted to spread the word in advance of their upcoming releases.

When you make news, it's important to tell the story journalists are most interested in first and foremost, but you can often squeeze in a little background of what you are most passionate about into your quotes. In this case, the winemaker's quote focuses attention on his experimentation with two styles of wine made from the exact same grapes. Without his context, most journalists wouldn't know the difference between a Durif and a Petite Sirah. Shesh!

We got the story told by the Napa Valley wine press and influential bloggers. Later on, they leveraged this coverage into a good review from Wine Spectator.

PRESS RELEASE:
TEAZLED.COM DADT GREETING CARDS

When I launched the LGBT greeting card company Teazled.com, they were positioned to be the first company in history to offer Coming Out cards specifically targeted to U.S. military personnel.

Sometimes when national news is made the local media looks for a way to bring it home to their audience. This is a perfect example of how you can "localize" a national story by piggy-backing your message into the news cycle.
